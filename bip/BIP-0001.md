## Abstract

This BIP establishes the initial governance charter for the Biological Sovereignty Protocol. It specifies that protocol-level upgrades — including changes to the on-chain modules, taxonomy root, and governance process itself — require approval from a 2-of-3 multisig. The Ambrósio Institute holds 2 of the 3 keys to steward the protocol; the protocol owner holds 1 of 3 to hold veto power over unilateral Institute action. Every governance action is recorded on-chain via the `governance.move` module and publicly auditable.

---

## Motivation

An open protocol without an explicit governance charter defaults to the governance of whoever controls deployment keys. This is unacceptable for infrastructure that custodies biological identity.

BSP needs a governance model that is:

- **Resistant to unilateral action** — no single party can force a breaking change.
- **Transparent** — every decision is recorded on-chain.
- **Bootstrappable** — works from day one without a pre-existing DAO.
- **Upgradeable** — the charter itself can be replaced by a future BIP under its own rules.

A 2-of-3 multisig is the simplest structure that meets these requirements at launch. It can be replaced by a richer scheme (elected council, token-gated DAO, citizen-weighted vote, etc.) through a future BIP-G accepted under the current charter.

## Specification

### Key distribution

| Key | Holder | Role |
|---|---|---|
| K1 | Ambrósio Institute — Primary | Institute steward (primary) |
| K2 | Ambrósio Institute — Secondary | Institute steward (secondary, cold-stored) |
| K3 | Protocol Owner | Independent veto holder |

Any 2 of the 3 keys are required to authorize a governance action. Institute-only actions (K1 + K2) are possible; the protocol owner (K3) can veto-block any Institute-only action by refusing to co-sign a counter-proposal — and can also pair with either K1 or K2 to act against an Institute action.

### Scope — actions requiring multisig approval

1. Deployment or upgrade of any Move module in the `bsp::` address space (BEO, IEO, Consent, Exchange, Taxonomy, Governance).
2. Acceptance of any BIP-P (protocol / schema) or BIP-G (governance) as `FINAL`.
3. Rotation of any of K1, K2, K3.
4. Changes to the IEO certification process (levels, auditors, revocation rules).
5. Emergency pause / unpause of protocol modules, limited to 72 hours per incident.
6. Changes to the Registry API relayer policy (gas funding, rate limits).

### Scope — actions NOT requiring multisig

- Acceptance of BIP-T (taxonomy) proposals, which are accepted by simple WG consensus + 30-day comment period.
- Documentation updates, examples, and SDK releases.
- Operational changes that do not alter on-chain state or the published spec.

### On-chain enforcement

All in-scope actions are authorized through the `governance.move` module. The module:

- Enforces the 2-of-3 signature requirement at the Move verifier level.
- Emits a `GovernanceAction` event for every authorized action.
- Stores the public keys K1, K2, K3 under the governance resource account.
- Rejects any transaction to the `bsp::*` address space that lacks a matching `GovernanceAction` authorization.

See the reference implementation at [`spec/move/governance.move`](../spec/move/governance.move).

### Key rotation

Rotating any of K1, K2, K3 requires the standard 2-of-3 approval. The outgoing key may not sign its own replacement; the rotation must be signed by the other two holders. This prevents a compromised key from rotating itself to an attacker-controlled key.

### Emergency pause

Any 2-of-3 may trigger a pause of protocol modules. Pause automatically expires after 72 hours. Extension requires a new 2-of-3 approval. No unbounded pause is possible.

### Charter replacement

This charter may be replaced by a future BIP-G accepted under its own rules. A replacement BIP must include:

- Full text of the new charter.
- Migration path for K1, K2, K3 to the new scheme.
- Activation block / timestamp.

## Rationale

### Why 2-of-3 and not 3-of-5 or 5-of-9?

At launch, a larger multisig is operationally expensive and does not materially improve security. 2-of-3 gives the protocol owner a true veto while preventing either Institute key alone from acting. When the protocol has broader institutional adoption, a future BIP-G can expand the signer set.

### Why does the Institute hold 2 of 3?

The Institute is the initial steward and bears operational responsibility for the protocol. Giving the Institute 2 keys lets it act on routine matters (e.g., certification updates, emergency pauses) without requiring coordination with the protocol owner for every action — while still preventing unilateral breaking changes, because the protocol owner can always pair with either Institute key or with an external actor in a future rotation.

### Why include a protocol owner as K3?

To prevent institutional capture. If the Institute ever acts against the interests of BEO holders, K3 can refuse to sign and block any BIP-P / BIP-G progression. K3 is an independent check.

### Alternatives considered

| Alternative | Reason rejected |
|---|---|
| Sole Institute control | Unacceptable for sovereign data infrastructure. Violates the protocol's core value proposition. |
| Token-gated DAO from launch | No credible token distribution at v1.0. Governance would be captured by early speculators. |
| 3-of-5 with external signers | No credible neutral third parties identified at launch. Adding weak signers reduces security. |
| Optimistic governance (timelock + veto) | Requires a credible veto community that does not yet exist. Revisit in a future BIP-G. |

## Backward Compatibility

- [x] This change is fully backward compatible

This BIP establishes the initial charter. No prior charter exists to migrate from.

---

## Implementation Notes

- `governance.move` module must be deployed at genesis.
- K1, K2, K3 public keys are committed to the module at deployment and cannot be changed except via the rotation procedure above.
- All other `bsp::*` modules MUST import `governance::authorize_action` and gate their upgrade entrypoints on a matching `GovernanceAction`.
- SDKs expose `bsp.governance.verifyAction(txHash)` for external verification.
